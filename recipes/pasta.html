Pasta

Description

Of all the classic pasta dishes, few are as deceptively simple or as richly satisfying as a well-made carbonara. At its heart, it's a beautiful alchemy of just a few key ingredients: hot, al dente pasta tossed vigorously with raw eggs, a generous amount of sharp Pecorino Romano cheese, and crispy cubes of guanciale. The residual heat from the pasta gently cooks the eggs into a smooth, creamy sauce that clings to every strand without the need for any actual cream. Finished with a heavy crack of black pepper, the final dish is a perfect balance of salty, savory, and peppery flavors, creating a comforting and deeply luxurious meal.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Guanciale
  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano
  • Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Heat pan. Cook guanciale until crispy.
  2. Boil pasta until al dente.
  3. Whisk eggs, cheese, and pepper. Drain pasta, reserve water.
  4. Combine pasta and guanciale. Remove from heat.
  5. Add egg mixture, stirring quickly. Thin with pasta water as needed.
  6. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.
