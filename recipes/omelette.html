Omellete

Description

The key to a perfect French omelette is low, gentle heat and constant, swift motion. Whisked eggs are poured into a generously buttered pan and agitated continuously with a fork to create small, soft curds throughout. Just before set, the omelette is rolled neatly into a tight oval or folded into thirds, resulting in a tender, slightly runny interior encased in a smooth, pale yellow exterior with no visible browning. It is a masterclass in technique, celebrating the pure, rich flavor of eggs and butter.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Whisk eggs thoroughly. Heat butter in a non-stick pan.
  3. Pour eggs into the pan. Stir gently with a fork.
  4. Let the eggs set slightly. Fold the omelette in half or into a roll.
  5. Slide onto a plate. Serve immediately.
